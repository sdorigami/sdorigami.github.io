Resources

A curated collection of origami instruction, inspiration, and community — for folders of every level.

Start Here

Learning to Fold

Whether you've never folded a thing or you're chasing complex crease patterns, these resources will meet you where you are.

Origami Resource Center

Step-by-step diagrams for beginners through advanced folders. One of the most comprehensive free collections online.

Origami Instructions

Clear photo-based instructions for hundreds of models. Great for beginners who prefer pictures over diagrams.

Tadashi Mori on YouTube

High-quality video tutorials ranging from simple to advanced. Exceptional production quality and clear instruction.

Origami Instructions (YouTube)

Hundreds of folding videos from easy to complex. A reliable go-to for following along model by model.
Community & Organizations

The Origami World

Connect with the wider origami community — national conventions, publications, and fellow enthusiasts.

OrigamiUSA

The national organization for origami in America. Annual convention, diagrams library, and membership community.

British Origami Society

One of the oldest origami societies in the world, with a rich publication history and active membership.

Gilad's Origami Page

An encyclopedic database of origami books, designers, and models. The definitive reference for serious folders.

Robert J. Lang — Origami

Website of one of the foremost origami artists and theorists in the world. Includes TreeMaker software and complex diagrams.
Go Deeper

Diagrams & Crease Patterns

For intermediate to advanced folders ready to tackle complex models and mathematical origami.

Origami Database

A searchable database of origami diagrams and crease patterns from designers around the world.

Origami on Flickr

Thousands of photos shared by folders globally — a great source of inspiration and community connection.

r/origami

An active community for sharing folds, asking questions, and discovering new models from folders around the world.

TreeMaker (Robert Lang)

Free software for designing your own origami bases mathematically. Used by serious designers worldwide.
Member Recommendations

Books We Love

These titles have inspired and educated our members over the years — including works by our own founders.

Florence Temko — Origami for Beginners

One of many beloved books by our co-founder Florence Temko, making origami accessible to all ages.

Robert J. Lang — Origami Design Secrets

The definitive book on the mathematical principles behind designing original origami models.

Peter Engel — Origami from Angelfish to Zen

A beautiful book exploring the philosophy, art, and craft of paper folding at a deep level.

Eric Gjerde — Origami Tessellations

The go-to guide for tessellation folding — geometric patterns that tile to create stunning surfaces.
