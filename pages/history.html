The Beginning

Where It All Started

The Greater San Diego Origami Group was founded in the early 1990s by two remarkable women whose passion for paper folding sparked a community that has flourished ever since.

Florence Temko was a prolific author who wrote dozens of books on origami and paper crafts, helping introduce the art to countless beginners in America and abroad. Her gift was making origami feel welcoming, joyful, and accessible to anyone willing to pick up a square of paper.

V'Ann Cornelius brought an artist's eye and a curator's vision. She co-curated Origami Masterworks, one of the first major museum-based origami exhibitions in the world, held at the Mingei International Museum in Balboa Park. Her work was internationally recognized, and her innovations in displaying origami as fine art helped elevate the medium in the eyes of the cultural world.

Together, they created a space where folders of all levels could gather, learn, and be inspired by one another. That spirit endures today.

Our Mission

What Drives Us

The group's mission has never changed from the day it was founded: to share the joy of paper folding, nurture its growth, bring people together, and encourage community among paper folders.

We welcome absolute beginners and experienced artists alike. What unites us isn't skill level — it's a shared wonder at what a single sheet of paper can become.

If you're in San Diego and curious about origami, you belong here.
